Inkscape

Often viewed as the hands-down definitive alternative to Illustrator, Inkscape feels like one of the most complete alternatives to date, perhaps even more so here than GIMP is to Photoshop.

About

First released in 2003, Inkscape looks like a very basic editor while hiding some pretty powerful features. Another extraordinarily stable program, Inkscape is capable of importing just about any file and offers a fantastic suite of tools.

As a Replacement...

To kick things off, Inkscape is capable of importing Illustrator files to a limited extent, which could be called a dig at Adobe or a gesture to anyone who downloads a .AI file without a program to modify it. Its default keyboard shortcuts are rather awkward (its toolbox requires keys ranging from M to F3 to Ctrl+F2), though its preferences go even further in acknowledging or antagonizing Adobe by offering the option to have its keyboard shortcuts follow Illustrator's (CS2's, at least). The essentials (such as a pen tool and gradients) are all there, along with some more advanced features like symbols.

Downsides

Inkscape works in SVG, and this is both a strength and a flaw; unfortunately, as Inkscape's wiki explains, multiple artboards (known in Inkscape as pages) don't currently exist in SVG and therefore can't be in Inkscape either. The program also lacks a color picker at the level of GIMP's or an Adobe program's, and this notably lowers the experience for me.

Inkscape also doesn't have a dark theme by default, but those in the community have contributed such themes on their own—one reason why I particularly like open source software.

Advantages

To be honest: load times. Inkscape doesn't even need a loading screen, instead fully loading on my computer within 6 seconds flat. Since most of my vector work is relatively simple, I'd been used to waiting on Adobe's wait times for quite a long time, so this came as a massive relief. Especially if you use a lower end PC, this is extremely convenient and definitely shouldn't be dismissed under the fact that it doesn't impact workflow.

The Bottom Line

Inkscape isn't complete, per se, but from opacity masks to text tools, it's come pretty close. Combining extremely fast loading times with powerful functionality, I've made Inkscape my go-to vector editing program even with my access to the Creative Cloud.
