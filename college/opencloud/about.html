About the Webmaster

Tom Pavlat is an independent web designer/developer who specializes in updating websites, logos and more. Tom is also a creative hobbyist, experienced in Creative Cloud programs since late 2013. An open source enthusiast, Tom has high hopes for its potential to match—and even surpass—propriety software.

Frequently Asked Questions

This is a confusing name. Couldn't “Cloud” refer to just about anything on the Web?

Well... yes. I personally would've preferred the name “Open Suite,” but Adobe doesn't call its programs the Creative Suite anymore (and, as an alternate name, “Creative Open” sounds just plain bad).

What's the point of this site?

As I'd mentioned on my home page, there are many, many, many, many resources that list free and even open source alternatives for the Creative Cloud, though few of them (if any) detail exactly what makes them good alternatives, or which features they're lacking, or even anything they may have that even Adobe's programs don't have. My site is aimed toward anyone deciding on whether to make the switch, enabling them to judge on their own terms whether the Cloud is still worth the price while giving them all the information they'll need to know on its alternative programs.

Not to complain, but there are only four programs here.

Well, yeah. As this started out a college project with a scrict deadline, this site isn't currently very big, let alone all-encompassing. With that said, I hope to make vast leaps in the future, eventually comprising a vast amount of the Creative Cloud, perhaps with multiple alternatives for each program.

How big will the site get?

That ultimately depends on how much experience I'll gain with Creative Cloud programs and their alternatives. I'm planning on skipping the mobile apps, although I would only consider the site “complete” once it lists alternatives for all the major Adobe programs, including Animate and Dreamweaver. Of course, that won't necessarily be the end—technology is a changing field, and as major updates for programs' alternatives are released, revisions to my pages will become necessary.

Finally, which programs did you use to make your site's assets?

Y'know, I started out designing the background in Photoshop, but then I just stopped midway through. There was no way I was going to be able to live with the irony.

All images for this site were created using GIMP and Inkscape, while all video on the site was made using Olive or a combination of it and Kdenlive. This actually helped immensely in gathering information on the features, abilities and potential drawbacks of the programs.
